John Luther “Casey” Jones was a engineer on the Illinois Central Railroad along with his fireman Sim Webb. In the middle of a record run, Casey encountered the caboose of a stalled freight. He managed to slow the train sufficiently that no passengers were killed, but he died of injuries from the crash. There is some controversy over whether Casey missed the flagger warning of the stalled freight, or if the flagger was not at his post.

After Casey’s death, Wallace Saunders (who greased and maintained Casey’s engine) wrote the first version of this ballad. It spread all along the I.C. and throughout America.
