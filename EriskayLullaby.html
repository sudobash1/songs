201 Eriskay Lullaby (The Mermaid’s Song)

transpose
font size

Another Hebridean folksong from Eriskay translated from Scots Gaelic by Marjory Kennedy-Fraser. It depicts a mermaid singing a lullaby.

[D]Horo ​[Bm]lady bhig. ​[A]Horo ​[Bm]eile,
[G]Horo ​[D]lady bhig. ​[A]Horo ​[D]eile
[D]Horo ​[Bm]lady bhig, ​[A]Horo ​[Bm]eile; ​my
[G]babe on a ​[D]curling green ​[A]wave, be thy ​[D]cradling.
[D]Horo ​[Bm]leanabain. ​[A]Horo ​[Bm]eile,
[G]Horo ​[D]leanabain. ​[A]Horo ​[D]eile
[D]Horo ​[Bm]leanabain. ​[A]Horo ​[Bm]eile; while ​the
[G]seagull and ​[D]swan for thy ​[A]curach are ​[D]caring.
[D]Horo ​[Bm]lady bhig. ​[A]Horo ​[Bm]eile,
[G]Horo ​[D]lady bhig. ​[A]Horo ​[D]eile
[D]Horo ​[Bm]lady bhig, ​[A]Horo ​[Bm]eile; with ​the
[G]lark in the ​[D]mornin’ ye’ll ​[A]rise again ​[D]early.
[D]Horo ​[Bm]leanabain. ​[A]Horo ​[Bm]eile,
[G]Horo ​[D]leanabain. ​[A]Horo ​[D]eile

Glossary:

eile
a lilting word pronounced “ay-lay”
lady bhig
lady small (pronounced: “laddie vik”)
lenabain
little child (pronounced: “LA-na-ban”)
curach
coracle, as the mermaid would use (pronounced: “coor-ak” with “k” as in Scots “loch”)
