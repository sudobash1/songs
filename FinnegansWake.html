120 Finnegan’s Wake

font size

This is a comic balid which dates back to the middle of the 19ᵗʰ century. It is most likely an American music hall song which has taken on another life as Irish folk music.

Amusingly, the work “whiskey” comes from the Gaelic words “uisce beatha,” meaning “water of life.” In this song, this etymology is taken literally.

And to [G]help him with his ​[C]work each ​day,
He’d a “[Am]drop of the craythur” ​[D]every ​[G]morn.

Glossary:

hod
a container to carry bricks in (or in slang usage: a tankard)
tipple
to drink alcohol
craythur
whiskey (pronounced: “KREH-thər”)
trotters
feet
full
drunk (ie full of whiskey)
wake
to remain with a body prior to burial or such a gathering
tay

mavourneen
darling (pronounced: “mə-VUR-een”)
gob
mouth
shillelagh
a walking stick made from blackthorn wood or oak that doubles as a cudgel (pronounced “shil-AY-lee”)

fight
thanum on dhoul
Irish Gaelic for “the devil take you” (pronounced “ta-NAM on dool”)
