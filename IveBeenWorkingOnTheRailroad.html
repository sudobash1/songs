507 I’ve Been Working on the Railroad

transpose

This song is actually an aggregate of two older folk songs. It seems to have been combined into its modern form around the end of the 19ᵗʰ century at Princeton University.

[C]I’ve been working on the ​railroad,

[F]Can’t you hear the captain ​[C]shouting:
“[G⁷]Dinah, blow your ​[C]horn.”
[C]Dinah, won’t you ​blow,
[F]Dinah, won’t you ​blow,
[G⁷]Dinah, won’t you blow your ​[C]horn.
[C]Dinah, won’t you ​blow,
[D⁷]Dinah, won’t you ​blow,
[G⁷]Dinah, won’t you blow your ​[C]horn.
