This song is set to a modern descendent of a very old tune called “Robin Cushie”, which goes back to at least the middle of the 18ᵗʰ century. The lyrics are surrounded by a great deal of folklore, despite being written in the mid 19ᵗʰ century. According to this folklore, the song describes a Jacobite rebel soldier who is to be executed after the battle of Culloden. He will travel home my the underworld route, while his freed compatriot will travel on foot.

Folklore aside, this song does not have contemporary Jacobite roots. A broadside published in the 1830’s titled “Flora’s Lament for her Charlie” shares the first verse and chorus with this song. This prior song is of Flora MacDonald grieving for Prince Charles. The modern lyrics are attributed to Lady John Scott, who also wrote the tune for Annie Laurie.
