109 Rattlin’ Roarin’ Willie

transpose
font size

This song seems to date back to at least the start of the 18ᵗʰ century. The first two verses were already in existance when Sir Robert Burns added the third. The original verses were about a musician who murdered another musician and was executed.

Burns changed the meaning of the song by making the third verse about William Dunbar, the president of the Crochallan Fencibles drinking club (of which Burns was a glad member).
