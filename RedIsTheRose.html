101 Red is the Rose

transpose
font size

This traditional Irish song is set to the same tune as the Scottish song “Loch Lomond”; although, its lyrics seem to be somewhat inspired by the old Scottish broadside “Flora’s Lament for her Charlie.”

Its earliest known version is from a recording in the 1930’s, and the lyrics commonly sung now are somewhat changed. The final verse originally referred to the singers loss of sister and mother, and the emigration of his “bonnie Irish lass.”
