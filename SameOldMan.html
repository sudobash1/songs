413 Same Old Man

transpose
font size

This song was originally a “play-party” song. When it originated, protestant religious practices in America often forbid dancing and playing of instruments. With clapping and movements that went along with the lyrics, these “play-parties” were social events where dancing was done under the guise of simple games.

As play-parties faded, their songs continued in the folk tradition, gathering and exchainging new verses. The owl verse in this rendition is originally from the English folk song Leather-Winged Bat.
