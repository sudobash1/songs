11 The Foggy Dew

This ballad was written by Fr Charles O’Neill about the Easter Rising in Ireland. This was during the First World War. Hundreds of thousands of Irishmen had joined the British forces during the war, but a group of Irish patriots used Brittan’s preoccupation with the war to launch a revolt.

The revolt failed, but the British retaliation afterwards only strengthened Irish nationalistic sentiment. The leaders of the uprising were executed, and several thousand rebels were sent to internment camps in Britain. As a result the Irish people elected members Sinn Féin party who declared independence, and the Irish War of Independence was fought soon after.

Suvla and Sud El Bar were locations in the failed British campaign against the Ottoman Empire. The British were justifying the war by insisting on the independence of nations assumed into the Ottoman Empire. This song points out the Irish nationalistic sentiment that Ireland should be independent of the British Empire. Patrick Pearse was the commander of the Irish forces in the Easter Rising. Cathal Brugha was a distinguished Irish combatant in the rising.
