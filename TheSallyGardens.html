This song was originally a poem by William Butler Yeats from the late 19ᵗʰ century. He had written it around a song fragment remembered by an elderly woman.

The garden referred to was likely a willow tree grove by a river. These trees would be referred to as Sallow (or in this case Sally) trees. Locals would use the supple branches for thatching their roofs.
