122 The Wind That Shakes the Barley

This song was written in the mid-19ᵗʰ century by Robert Dwyer Joyce about two lovers during the Irish Rebellion of 1798. The United Irishmen was the name of the group rebelling against British rule in Ireland. They had a significant victory at Oulart Hill which is referenced in the final verse.

The barley imagery in this song is often associated with Irish revolution. Barley grain was carried by the rebel solderers as food during travels, and when they were buried, the grain reportedly sprouted from their pockets. As the barley would grow again, year upon year, it became know to symbolize the heartiness of the Irish nationalistic spirit.
