transpose
font size

This song’s origins are from a ballad about the notorious highwayman Patrick Flemming which was written around the time of his execution in 1650. (In all likelyhood it was written to be printed and sold to the croud gathered for the execution.) Unlike in the song, it was a landlord who betrayed Flemming; however, the part about filling the pistols with water is accurate. Pistols of the time would use loose black-powder, which, if it got wet, would be unable to fire.

In the 1850’s, broadsides containing the modern version of the song were published in Glasgow with the note: “This song is a regular nightly favourite in the Glasgow Saloons and it meets with its regular nightly applause.”
